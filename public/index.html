Epley maneuver

A guided walk-through for vertigo.

This talks you through the Epley maneuver step by step and uses your phone's motion sensors to sense when you've reached each position — so you can keep your eyes closed and just listen.

Before you start. This is for people whose doctor has already diagnosed BPPV and told them which ear is affected. It is not a diagnosis or a substitute for medical care.
  • Do it on a bed, with a pillow ready for under your shoulders.
  • Have someone nearby if you can.
  • Stop and call your doctor for chest pain, weakness, numbness, or trouble speaking.

Setup · 2 of 3

The plan: three moves.

About two minutes. You'll roll your head through a set sequence to guide the loose crystals in your ear back where they belong. It really is mostly rolling until you're facing the floor.

  1. 1
    Lie backhead stays turned to the side
  2. 2
    Turn your headthe other way, part-way toward the floor
  3. 3
    Roll onto your sideuntil you're looking down at the floor

Then you sit up. Each position is held for 30 seconds. I'll speak every move and beep when it's time — once the phone's at your head, you won't touch it again.

Setup · 3 of 3

Turn your sound up.

The guide talks you through every move so you can close your eyes. It only works if you can hear it — turn your phone's volume up, take it off silent, then test it.
